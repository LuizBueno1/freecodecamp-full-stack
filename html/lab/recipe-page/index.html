How to Make a Perfect Condensed Milk Flan

Hello and welcome to our recipe page! We're thrilled to have you here. If you're a dessert lover or just someone who enjoys a sweet treat every now and then, you're in the right place! Today, we’ll guide you through making a classic and irresistible flan. This smooth and creamy dessert is a favorite in many households and perfect for any celebration—or just because you deserve a treat! So, grab your apron, and let’s dive into this delightful recipe together.

Ingredients

    For the caramel:

  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1/4 cup of water

    • for the flan:

  • 1 can of sweetened condensed milk (14 oz or 395 g)
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Caramel
    • In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the sugar and water. Stir gently until the sugar dissolves.
    • Let it cook without stirring until the mixture turns a golden amber color. Be careful not to let it burn.
    • Quickly pour the caramel into a round baking pan (about 8-9 inches in diameter), tilting the pan to coat the bottom evenly. Set aside to cool and harden.
  2. Make the Flan Mixture
    • In a blender, combine the sweetened condensed milk, whole milk, eggs, and vanilla extract (if using). Blend for 1-2 minutes until smooth and well mixed.
    • Strain the mixture through a fine sieve into the caramel-coated pan. This step ensures a silky texture by removing any egg lumps.
  3. Bake the Flan
    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C).
    • Place the baking pan in a larger baking dish. Fill the larger dish with hot water, creating a water bath (bain-marie). This helps the flan cook evenly and prevents cracking.
    • Bake for about 1 hour or until the flan is set but still slightly jiggly in the center.
  4. Cool and Serve
    • Remove the pan from the water bath and let it cool to room temperature.
    • Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for the best texture and flavor.
    • To serve, run a knife around the edges of the flan to loosen it. Place a plate on top of the pan and carefully invert it. The caramel will flow over the flan, creating a beautiful topping.
